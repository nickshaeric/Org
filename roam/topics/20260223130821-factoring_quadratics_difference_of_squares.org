#+title: Factoring quadratics: Difference of squares
#+filetags: :topic:
* Definition
- Every polynomial that is a difference of squares can be factored by applying the following formula: \(a^2 - b^2 = (a + b)(a - b)\)

* Key Points
- Factoring a polynomial involves writing it as a product of two or more polynomials.
  - It reverses the process of polynomial multiplication.

* Examples
** Example 1
Factoring \(x^2 - 16\)
   Both \(x^{2}\) and \(16\) are perfect squares, since \(x^2 = (x)^2\) and \(16 = (4)^2\).

   Therefore, \(x^2 - 16 = (x)^2 - (4)^2\)

   Since two sqaures are being subtracted, we can see that this polynomial
   represents a /difference of squares/.

   Therefore, \(x^2 - 16 = (x+4)(x-4)\)
