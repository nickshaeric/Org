#+title: Factoring quadratics: Perfect squares
#+filetags: :topic:
* Key Points
- Factoring a polynomial involves writing it as a product of two polynomials.
  - It reverses the process of polynomial multiplication.

** Intro: Factoring perfect sqaure trinomials
To expand any binomial, we can apply one of the following patterns.
- \((a + b)^{2} = a^{2} + 2ab + b^{2}\)
- \((a - b)^{2} = a^{2} - 2ab + b^{2}\)

The reverse of this expansion process is a form of factoring.
Pattern for factoring polynomials of the form \(a^2 \pm 2ab + b^2\):
- \(a^2 + 2ab + b^2 = (a + b)^2\)
- \(a^2 - 2ab + b^2 = (a - b)^2\)
