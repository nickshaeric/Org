#+title: Prime & Composite
#+filetags: :topic:
* Definition
** Prime Number
An integer $n$ is prime $\iff n>1$ so that its only positive divisors are $1$ and itself.

** Composite Number
An integer $n$ is composite $\iff n>1$ and $n$ is not prime.

* Key Points
- Smallest prime number is $2$.
- $2$ is the *only even prime*.
- Every integer $n>1$ is either prime or composite.
- If $n$ is composite, then it can be written as a product of two integers
  $a,b$ where $1<a<n$ and $1<b<n$.
- If $p$ is prime and $p \mid ab$, then $p \mid a$ or $p \mid b$.

* Examples
** $\forall x \in \mathbb{Z}, n^2+n+41$ is prime.
Answer: False

Proof: Let $n=40$, where $40 \in \mathbb{Z}$.
Then,
\begin{align*}
n^{2}+n+41 &= {(40)}^{2}+(40)+41 \\
&= 40(40+1)+41 \\
&= 40 \times 41+41 \\
&= 41^{2}
\end{align*}

$n^2+n+41$ is not a prime number.
