#+title: Composing Functions
#+filetags: :topic:
* Definition
In general, to indicate function \(f\) composed with function \(g\), we can write \(f \circ g\),
read as "\(f\) composed with \(g\)".

This composition is defined by the following rule:

\((f \circ g)(x) = f(g(x))\)

* Key Points
\((f \circ g)(x) \neq (g \circ f)(x)\)

* Examples
** Example 1
\(g(x)=x+4\)
\(h(x) = x^2 - 2x\)

Find \((h \circ g)(x)\):
\begin{align*}
h(g(x)) &= (x+4)^{2} - 2(x + 4) \\
        &= (x + 4)(x + 4) - 2x - 8 \\
        &= x^{2} + 4x + 4x + 16 - 2x - 8 \\
        &= x^{2} + 6x + 8
\end{align*}

** Example 2
If \(f(x) = 3x - 1\) and \(g(x) = x^3 + 2\), then what is \(f(g(3))\)$?

\begin{align*}
f(g(3)) &= 3(x^{3} + 2) - 1 \\
&= 3x^{3} + 6 - 1 \\
&= 3x^{3} + 5 \\
&= 3(3)^{3} + 5 \\
&= 3(27) + 5 \\
&= 86
\end{align*}
