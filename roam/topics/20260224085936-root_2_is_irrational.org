#+title: Root 2 Is Irrational
#+filetags: :topic:
* Proof
Prove \(\sqrt{2} \notin \mathbb{Q}\)

Proof by contraditcion.
Suppose \(\sqrt{2}\) is rational, that is \(\sqrt{2}=\dfrac{m}{n}\), where \(m\) and \(n\) are integers,
\(n \ne 0\) and \(gcd{(m,n)}=1\).

Then,
\begin{align*}
\sqrt{2} &= \frac{m}{n} \\
\left(\sqrt{2}\right)^{2} &= \frac{m^{2}}{n^{2}} \\
2 &= \frac{m^{2}}{n^{2}} \\
2n^{2} &= m^{2}
\end{align*}

\(m^{2}=2n^{2}\) shows that \(m^{2}\) is even.
Hence \(m\) is even, which is \(m=2k\) for some integer \(k\).

\begin{align*}
2n^{2} &= {(2k)}^{2} \\
2n^{2} &= 4k^{2} \\
\frac{2n^{2}}{2} &= \frac{4k^{2}}{2} \\
n^{2} &= 2k^{2}
\end{align*}

\(n^{2}=2k^{2}\) shows that \(n^{2}\) is even.
Hence \(n\) is even, which contradicts \(\gcd{(m,n)}=1\).

Thus, \(\sqrt{2} \notin \mathbb{Q}\).
