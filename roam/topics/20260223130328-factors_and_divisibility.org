#+title: Factors and Divisibility
#+filetags: :topic:
* Definition
Monomial: An expression that is the product of constants and nonnegative integer powers of \(x\), like \(3x^{2}\).

Polynomial: An expression that consists of a sum of monomials, like \(3x^2 + 6x - 1\).

* Key Points
When two or more polynomials are multiplied, we call each of these polynomials factors of the product.

In general, to determine whether one polynomial \(p\) is divisible by another polynomial \(q\), or equivalently whether \(q\) is a factor of \(p\), we can find and examine \(\dfrac{p(x)}{q(x)}\).
** Factoring quadratics: leading coefficient = 1
Factoring a polynomial involves expressing it is as a product of two or more polynomials.

*** Summary
In general, to factor a trinomial of the form \(x^2 + bx + c\), we need to find factors of \(c\) such that their sum is \(b\).

** Sum-product pattern
\begin{align*}
(x +m)(x+n) &= (x +m)(x) + (x+m)(n) \\
&= x^{2} + mx + nx  + m \cdot n \\
&= x^{2} + m(n + x) + m \cdot n
\end{align*}

The resulting equation:
\((x+m)(x+n) = x^{2} + (m \cdot n)x + m \cdot n\)
