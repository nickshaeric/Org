#+title: Cybersecurity
#+filetags: :lecture:
* Lecture Notes
Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting digital systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access, damage, or theft.

It is a multidisciplinary field - meaning it does not concern itself only with technical solutions, but equally with human factors such as user behaviour, organizational policy, and training.

The goals of cybersecurity are captured by the [[id:326ef9ea-d7ec-4034-967f-3465f91e8aa4][CIA Triad]]: confidentiality, integrity, and availability.
These three principles underlie the design, assessment, and management of virtually every security framework and risk management strategy in use today.

* Key Concepts
** Definition
*Cybersecurity* is the practice of protecting digital systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access, damage, or theft through a combination of technology and human-centered controls.
** Domains of Cybersecurity
Cybersecurity is not a single discipline but a collection of interconnected domains, each addressing a different surface of attack.
*Network Security* protects internal networks from intrusion by securing both hardware and software infrastructure.
*Application Security* ensures that software and devices remain free of threats through secure coding practices, regular updates, and rigorous testing.
*Information Security* protects the integrity and privacy of data both in storage and in transit - closely related to the [[id:326ef9ea-d7ec-4034-967f-3465f91e8aa4][CIA Triad]].
*Operational Security (OpSec)* governs the policies and procedures for handling data assets, including permissions, access levels, and authentication mechanisms.
*Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery Planning* ensures that organizations can continue operations and recover quickly after a security incident or natural disaster.

* Examples
** Common Types of Cyberattacks
*Malware* is a broad category of malicious software — including viruses, worms, spyware, and trojans - that disrupts, damages, or gains unauthorized access to systems.
*Phishing* is a social engineering attack in which an attacker impersonates a legitimate entity to deceive users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords or financial details, typically via email or fake websites.
*Ransomware* is a specific type of malware that encrypts a victim's data and demands payment for its restoration. It directly attacks the [[id:326ef9ea-d7ec-4034-967f-3465f91e8aa4][CIA Triad]] by violating both confidentiality and availability.
*DoS/DDoS (Denial-of-Service / Distributed Denial-of-Service)* floods a system or network with excessive traffic to render it unavailable to legitimate users. A DDoS attack coordinates this flood across many compromised systems simultaneously.
