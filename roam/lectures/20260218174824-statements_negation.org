#+title: Statements & Negation
#+filetags: :lecture:
* Lecture Notes
** Definitions
A /Statement/ = A meaningful sentence that is either true or false.

A /Proof/ = An explanation of why a statement is true or false.

/Negation/ = The operation that takes a statement and produces its opposite.

If a statement $P$ is true, its negation, denoted $\neg P$, is false, and vice versa;
often represented in logical terms as $\neg P \equiv \text{not} P$.
