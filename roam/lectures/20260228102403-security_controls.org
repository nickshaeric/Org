#+title: Security Controls
#+filetags: :lecture:
* Lecture Notes
Security controls are safeguards or countermeasures used to reduce risk, protect assets, and ensure the Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability of information as defined by the [[id:326ef9ea-d7ec-4034-967f-3465f91e8aa4][CIA Triad]].

They are the building blocks of any [[id:2f03d2a4-1d76-4da6-a7a1-492c3090953f][Defense-in-Depth]] strategy and are classified into three functional types based on *when* they act relative to a threat: before (preventive), during or after (detective), or after (corrective).

The key insight is that all three types are necessary - relying only on prevention is insufficient because no defense is perfect, and relying only on detection and correction means threats will always cause some degree of damage before being addressed.

The lecture's memorable formulation captures the relationship perfectly: "Prevention is ideal, detection is essential, correction is survival. A good security program has all three: stop it, spot it, and fix it."

* Key Concepts
** Preventive Controls
*Preventive controls* act *before an incident* by stopping threats at the entry point through blocking, restricting, or hardening systems.
They are proactive and reduce the likelihood of an attack or unauthorized access succeeding.
Examples:
- *Firewalls* - block unwanted traffic from reaching the internal network. See [[id:41771f87-ae23-43c3-a3fe-548cb4d38315][Firewalls]].
- *MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication)* - adds an extra layer of identity verification.
- *Encryption* - ensures intercepted data remains unreadable without a decryption key, directly protecting *Confidentiality* in the [[id:326ef9ea-d7ec-4034-967f-3465f91e8aa4][CIA Triad]].
- *Security Awareness Training* - educates users to recognize and avoid phishing and social engineering.
Key benefit: Reduces attack surface and stops many threats at the perimeter.

** Detective Controls
*Detective controls* act *during or after an incident* by monitoring systems and networks to identify malicious activity, violations, or abnormal behavior.
They do not block threats, but enable rapid awareness and response.
Examples:
- *IDS (Intrusion Detection Systems)* - monitor traffic and generate alerts. See [[id:018f7a00-e51d-4a05-865b-8f4933b6cb55][IDS & IPS]].
- *Audit Logs* - track and record system events for later review or forensic investigation.
- *SIEM Tools* - aggregate and analyze logs in real time to detect coordinated or distributed attacks across the environment.
Key benefit: Enables quick detection and facilitates incident response and forensic analysis.

** Corrective Controls
*Corrective controls* act *after an incident* by mitigating impact, restoring normal operations, and preventing recurrence.
They work alongside or after detective controls, focusing on damage control and recovery.
Examples:
- *System Reimaging* - reinstalls a clean OS version after malware infection.
- *Backups and Recovery Tools* - restore lost or corrupted data after ransomware or accidental deletion; this is why the [[id:00bead97-065c-4a3b-9b43-d98a4e5cb1c2][WannaCry Ransomware (2017)]]  impact was so severe for organizations without backups.
- *Isolation Protocols* - quarantine affected systems to prevent lateral spread of malware across the [[id:a4c39818-dd36-4f8a-ae85-a0e8c294ac09][distributed system]].
- *Patch Deployment* - after identifying exploited vulnerabilities, patches are applied to close the gap and prevent recurrence.
Key benefit: Minimizes business disruption and restores Availability in the [[id:326ef9ea-d7ec-4034-967f-3465f91e8aa4][CIA Triad]] after a breach.

* Examples
** Controls Across the Attack Lifecycle
*** Control Type
**** Preventative
Timing: Before incident
Examples: Firewall, MFA, encryption, training
CIA Pillar Protected: Confidentiality, Availability
**** Detective
Timing: During/After
Examples: IDS, audit logs, SIEM
CIA Pillar Protected: All three (via visibility)
**** Corrective
Timing: After incident
Examples: Backups, reimaging, isolation
CIA Pillar Protected: Availability, Integrity

** Equifax Through the Controls Lens
The [[id:e0707681-afc9-45c5-b207-1871161d0d2d][Equifax Data Breach (2017)]] failed at all three levels: no patch (preventive failure), no monitoring that detected lateral movement for months (detective failure), and no encryption at rest or network segmentation to limit damage (corrective/containment failure).
