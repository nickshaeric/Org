* Course Info
- Instructor: Dr. Rafee Al Ahsan
- Term: Winter 2026
* Course Overview
CPSC329 builds a foundational understanding of information security and privacy.
The course covers five core areas: recognizing threats and evaluating defenses in distributed systems, access control mechanisms, network and software-based attacks, ethical and legal issues in cybersecurity, and cryptographic tools and their applications.
The emphasis throughout is on thinking critically about real-world challenges in protecting digital systems.
* How the Course Operates
Lectures introduce key concepts to the whole class. Weekly tutorials reinforce lecture content through hands-on worksheets and demonstrations.
Worksheets must be completed *individually*, *in-person*, and *during your registered tutorial session only*.
The course concludes with a *Group Research Project and Presentation*.
* Assessments
** Grading Breakdown
- Tutorial Worksheets: *25%*
- Midterm Exam: *20%*
- Final Exam: *30%* (minimum 40% required to pass the course)
- Group Project & Presentation: *20%*
- Attendance: *5%*
** Midterm
A 2-hour, closed-book, in-person exam on *Saturday, February 28, 2026*.
If missed with valid documentation submitted within 24 hours, the midterm weight may be transferred to the final exam, bringing it to 50% of the course grade.
** Final Exam
A 3-hour, closed-book, in-person exam scheduled by the Registrar.
A minimum score of 40% on the final exam is required to pass the course regardless of other grades.
There are no make-ups or alternative arrangements under any circumstances.
** Worksheets
Completed individually, in-person, during your registered tutorial session only.
AI tools and external resources are strictly prohibited during worksheets.
Missing a worksheet without a valid documented reason results in a zero for both the worksheet and attendance that day.
** Attendance
Recorded for tutorials only. A minimum of *95%* attendance is required for full credit.
